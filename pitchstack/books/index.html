    Books

    Sprint: How to Solve Big Problems and Test New Ideas in Just Five Days

    by Jake Knapp from Google Ventures

    There are numerous lessons here for messaging in your pitch, testing your assumptions, and working with end-users to validate your idea.

    Also see Knapp's Medium post "The Three-Hour Brand Sprint"

    Start With Why

    by Simon Sinek

    Sinek's manta "people don't buy what you do, they buy why you do it" is explained in this book. A video idea is on TED, and is the the third most popular TED video of all time. It's an excellent, excellent read.

    Resonate

    By Nancy Duarte

    Duarte has many great books and presentations. In Resonate she gets to the heart of how a great presentation can both persuade and inspire.

    Made to Stick

    by Chip Heath and Dan Heath

    What makes an idea memorable, transferable, and enduring? If you want to change the world, you are going to have to structure your communication to be as sticky as possible.

    Influence

    by Dr. Robert Cialdini

    There is a science to persuasion, and ultimately getting someone to say "yes." Dr. Cialdini is an expert in the psychology of influence and persuasion and can give us all insights into how the human brain evaluates new ideas.

    Hooked

    by Nir Eyal

    We all get hooked on things like casual games or social media sites. In the age of the app there are plenty of examples—Facebook, Candy Crush, Tinder—that hook us into a habit. What exactly makes us get hooked on something?

    A New Brand World

    by Scott Bedbury

    Bedbury helped make Nike and Starbucks two of the most valuable brands in the world. His message is about setting out the principles that helped these companies become leaders in their respective industries, and was a precursor to Simon Sinek's Start With Why.

    To Sell is Human

    by Daniel H. Pink

    Pitching is basically sales, and in this book Pink describes the six successors to the elevator pitch, the three rules for understanding someone else's perspective, the five frames that can make your message clearer and more persuasive.

    Crossing the Chasm

    by Geoffrey Moore
